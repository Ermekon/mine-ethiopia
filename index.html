Investment Case

Why Ethiopia

Africa's most mineral-rich frontier, with a reformed legal framework and one-stop-shop licensing designed for institutional investment.

Protected by Law

Proclamation 678/2010 encodes 100% foreign ownership and ICSID arbitration into statute. Your investment is protected by the same legal architecture that governs World Bank-financed projects.

›
  • 100% foreign ownership in large-scale mining — no joint-venture requirement
  • Full profit repatriation with no transfer restrictions or approval delays
  • International dispute resolution under ICSID — not domestic courts
  • BITs with 19 countries including Germany, France, UK, and China
  • Digital cadastre creates a transparent, auditable license record
Underexplored at Scale

Less than 15% of Ethiopia's known mineral-bearing zones have been drilled to commercial depth. Over 200 metric tons of gold identified. Tantalum output ranks 6th globally — and the surface has barely been scratched.

›
  • Gold: >200 metric tons confirmed — Adola, Lege Dembi, Shakiso belts
  • Tantalum: world's 6th largest producer from a single site (Kenticha)
  • Potash: among Africa's largest known deposits in the Afar (Dallol)
  • Gemstones: Wollo Opal commands $1,500–$15,000/carat at auction
  • 1.14 million km² — less explored than Western Australia in 1970
One Door, 16 Agencies

Ethiopia's one-stop-shop model is not a metaphor — it is a codified coordination mandate. MoMP routes all inter-agency requirements. You submit once. Sixteen agencies respond in parallel.

›
  • MoMP as primary authority — single point of submission for all license types
  • EIC handles investment incentives, registration, and facilitation in parallel
  • GSE provides geological data access within the same process
  • ERCA clears customs and tax simultaneously — no sequential queuing
  • Digital portal tracks status across all 16 agencies in real time
Economics Built for Returns

25% corporate income tax. 100% exploration cost recovery off the top. Five-year customs exemption on all capital equipment. This is a fiscal regime designed to make the numbers work at the exploration stage — not just at production.

›
  • 25% flat corporate income tax — confirmed under Proclamation 802/2013
  • 100% exploration cost recovery before any profit is computed
  • 5-year customs duty exemption on machinery, equipment, and spare parts
  • Loss carry-forward up to 10 years — covers the full exploration cycle
  • VAT exemption on mining machinery and direct production inputs

Regulatory Framework

7 License Types

Proclamation 678/2010 establishes seven mineral license categories. Each grants specific rights, durations, and area entitlements. Click any card to expand details.

1
Reconnaissance License
Duration: 1 year · Renewable once

Non-exclusive surface-level survey rights. Allows geological mapping, sampling, and geophysical surveys without drilling.

Eligibility: Open to all registered entities · No minimum capital requirement Apply via Cadastre Portal →
2
Exploration License
Duration: 3 + 2 + 2 years (with renewals)

Exclusive drilling and sampling rights within a defined area. Most common entry point for institutional exploration companies.

Eligibility: Corporate registration · Work program + EIA required · Financial capacity proof Apply via Cadastre Portal →
3
Development License
Duration: 3 years

Covers the feasibility, engineering, and construction phase between exploration completion and commercial production.

Eligibility: Prior exploration license holder · Feasibility study required Apply via Cadastre Portal →
4
Mining License (Large Scale)
Duration: Up to 20 years · Renewable

Full commercial extraction rights for large-scale operations. Grants exclusive mining rights within the licensed area and all fiscal benefits.

Eligibility: Development license or feasibility study · Min. capital per MoMP scale thresholds · Full EIA Apply via Cadastre Portal →
5
Small Scale Mining License
Duration: Up to 10 years · Renewable

Commercial extraction rights for smaller operations. Reduced capital requirements, simplified EIA process.

Eligibility: Individual or small enterprise · Simplified work program required Apply via Cadastre Portal →
6
Artisanal Mining License
Duration: 2 years · Renewable

Rights for traditional, labour-intensive extraction by Ethiopian nationals. Forms the base of the country's informal sector formalization strategy.

Eligibility: Ethiopian nationals only · Cooperative or individual registration Apply via Cadastre Portal →
7
Mineral Processing License
Duration: Up to 10 years · Renewable

Required for cutting, polishing, refining, or any value-added processing of minerals. Can be held independently of mining rights.

Eligibility: Open to all registered entities · Processing facility plan required Apply via Cadastre Portal →

Investor Questions

Frequently Asked

The questions every investor asks in their first meeting with MoMP — answered here so you arrive prepared.

Can foreigners own 100% of a mining company in Ethiopia? ›
Yes. Proclamation 678/2010 explicitly permits 100% foreign ownership in large-scale and exploration mining. There is no mandatory joint-venture requirement with the government or local partners. This applies to corporate entities registered in Ethiopia.
What happens if I miss a license renewal deadline? ›
An exploration license not renewed within the grace period may be cancelled and the area returned to the cadastre as available for new applications. MoMP issues a formal notice before cancellation. Active companies with a work program in progress can apply for a 30-day extension — contact the Licensing Directorate before the deadline expires.
Is capital equipment import tax-free? ›
Yes, for the first five years of operation. All capital equipment, machinery, spare parts, and vehicles directly used in mining operations are exempt from customs duty under the 5-year customs duty exemption incentive. After five years, standard customs rates apply unless further incentive agreements are negotiated with EIC.
Can I repatriate profits in foreign currency? ›
Yes. Ethiopia permits full profit repatriation in foreign currency for licensed mining operations. The National Bank of Ethiopia (NBE) regulations permit foreign currency retention accounts for export-earning mining companies. Repatriation is processed through licensed commercial banks.
What is the minimum area I can apply for under an exploration license? ›
Exploration licenses are granted for areas defined by GPS coordinates up to a maximum of 1,500 km² for a single license. There is no enforced minimum area. Multiple non-overlapping licenses can be held by the same entity. Initial area selection is done through the Digital Cadastre Portal.
Is there an international dispute resolution mechanism? ›
Yes. Proclamation 678/2010 provides for international arbitration under ICSID (International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes). This is in addition to Ethiopia's BITs with 19 countries. Investors are not required to use domestic courts for investment disputes — ICSID awards are internationally enforceable.
